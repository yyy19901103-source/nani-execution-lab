1. SCOPE OF SUPPLY
1.1 The Vendor shall supply one (1) complete centrifugal compressor package
    including but not limited to:
    a) Centrifugal compressor body (barrel-type, horizontally split casing
       not acceptable for this service)
    b) Integrally geared speed increasing gear unit
    c) Lube oil console (integrated on common base frame)
    d) Dry gas seal system (both inboard and outboard)
    e) Process gas coolers (two inter-stage + one discharge)
    f) Steam turbine driver
    g) Inlet filter/separator with automatic drain
    h) Anti-surge control valve and recycle line (within package boundary)
    i) Local control panel (LCP) with PLC
    j) Machine monitoring system (MMS) per API 670
    k) Coupling and coupling guard (between compressor and gearbox,
       and between gearbox and steam turbine)
    l) Common base frame, anchor bolts, and leveling pads
    m) All instrumentation, valves, and piping within package battery limit
    n) Installed spare rotor (fully assembled with seals)
    o) Special tools and commissioning spare parts (24 months operation)

1.2 The following items are excluded from Vendor scope but shall be coordinated:
    a) Lube oil piping beyond package battery limit
    b) Seal gas supply piping from plant utility header
    c) Condensate drain collection header
    d) Electrical power cables beyond local junction box
    e) DCS (Purchaser-supplied; Vendor to provide signal interface list)

2. APPLICABLE CODES AND STANDARDS
2.1 Compressor design and manufacture: API 617, 8th Edition (Chapters 1 and 2).
    All requirements of API 617 shall be met unless specifically noted herein.
2.2 Steam turbine driver: API 611, 5th Edition (general-purpose industrial
    steam turbines up to 750 kW) or API 612, 3rd Edition as applicable.
2.3 Gearbox: API 613, 5th Edition.
2.4 Lube oil system: API 614, 4th Edition.
2.5 Dry gas seals: API 692, 1st Edition.
2.6 Machine monitoring: API 670, 5th Edition.
2.7 Pressure vessels (gas coolers, KO drums): ASME Section VIII Division 1,
    with full U-stamp certification.
2.8 Flanges: ASME B16.5. No JIS or other flange standards will be accepted
    for nozzle connections.
2.9 Electrical area classification: Class I, Division 2, Group D (NEC)
    for all electrical equipment within 7.5 m of the compressor package.
    IEC 60079 equivalent (Zone 2) is acceptable only with written approval.
2.10 Welding: ASME Section IX. All weld procedures and welder qualifications
     shall be submitted for Purchaser's review before commencement of welding.
2.11 Material certification: EN 10204 Type 3.2 for all pressure-containing parts
     and rotating components. Type 3.1 is not acceptable for rotor components.
2.12 NACE MR0175/ISO 15156 compliance is required for all wetted parts, as
     the gas stream contains up to 15 ppm H2S.
2.13 Vendor shall list all deviations from the above codes in a Deviation Register,
     submitted at bid stage and updated at order placement.

3. PROCESS AND DESIGN BASIS
3.1 Service                   : Ethylene plant offgas (C2/C3 mixture)
3.2 Gas composition (mol%)    :
    Ethylene       42.5%
    Ethane         18.3%
    Propylene      15.1%
    Propane         8.4%
    Hydrogen        6.2%
    Methane         5.1%
    Others          4.4%
3.3 Molecular weight          : 33.4 (design) / 35.0 (maximum, fouling condition)
3.4 Suction conditions (1st stage):
    Flow               : 85,000 Nm³/h (normal) / 95,000 Nm³/h (maximum)
    Temperature        : 40°C
    Pressure           : 1.2 barg
3.5 Interstage conditions     : As determined by Vendor thermodynamic calculation;
    Purchaser to review and approve before detailed design.
3.6 Discharge conditions      :
    Pressure           : 18.5 barg
    Temperature        : 55°C (maximum, after final cooler)
3.7 Driver power (estimated)  : 4,200 kW (Vendor to confirm by calculation)
3.8 Design ambient temperature : 35°C (normal) / 42°C (maximum) / 5°C (minimum)
3.9 Relative humidity         : up to 95% non-condensing
3.10 Seismic zone             : Zone 2B per UBC 1997. Vendor shall confirm
     package natural frequencies do not coincide with seismic frequency range.

4. COMPRESSOR MECHANICAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 The compressor shall be a two-section, integrally geared centrifugal
    compressor. Inline (single-shaft) configuration will not be accepted.
4.2 Rotor dynamic stability analysis (Level 2 per API 617 Annex I) shall be
    performed and submitted for Purchaser's review.
4.3 All critical speeds shall be at least 15% away from the operating speed
    range (5% margin from trip speed and 105% of maximum continuous speed).
4.4 Maximum allowable vibration (unfiltered, peak-to-peak) at rated conditions:
    25.4 µm or 30% of the alert setpoint per API 670, whichever is less.
4.5 Maximum bearing temperature (white metal): 110°C at rated conditions.
4.6 Impellers shall be three-dimensional (3D) milled from a single billet of
    AISI 17-4PH (H1025 condition) stainless steel. No welded impellers.
4.7 Rotor assembly shall be balanced to ISO 1940/1 Grade G1.0 (not G2.5).
4.8 Rotor shall withstand an overspeed test at 115% of maximum continuous
    speed with no permanent deformation.
4.9 Minimum distance between impellers and casing walls shall be confirmed
    by FEA (Finite Element Analysis); results to be submitted for review.
4.10 All gas-side O-rings shall be of Viton (FKM) material. PTFE-encapsulated
     O-rings are not acceptable.
4.11 Compressor inlet and discharge nozzles shall be top-entry (both inlet and
     outlet from the top of casing) to facilitate piping layout.
     Bottom-entry discharge nozzle requires written deviation approval.

5. GEARBOX REQUIREMENTS
5.1 Gearbox shall be of the integrally geared type, with a service factor of
    minimum 1.25 on rated power.
5.2 Gear tooth contact pattern shall be checked and documented during assembly.
5.3 Gearbox oil mist lubrication is not acceptable. Forced-feed lubrication
    from the shared lube oil console is required.
5.4 Vendor shall confirm gearbox noise level does not exceed 85 dB(A) at 1 m
    distance under full-load operating condition.

6. STEAM TURBINE DRIVER REQUIREMENTS
6.1 Steam turbine type: condensing or backpressure, as determined by Vendor's
    thermodynamic optimization. Purchaser to review turbine selection.
6.2 Inlet steam conditions: 42 barg / 400°C (superheat).
    Minimum inlet steam quality: 99.5% dryness.
6.3 Exhaust steam condition: 4.5 barg (backpressure) or condensing to 0.1 bara.
    Vendor to propose optimum cycle and justify in technical bid.
6.4 Governor shall be an electronic speed governor (ESG) with remote speed
    setpoint capability via 4-20 mA signal from DCS.
6.5 Steam turbine trip functions shall be hardwired (not via DCS) and shall
    include as a minimum: overspeed, low lube oil pressure, high vibration,
    high axial displacement, and high exhaust temperature.
6.6 The turbine shall be capable of motoring (driving the compressor at low
    load) without condensate accumulation in the exhaust.
6.7 Turning gear shall be supplied for cool-down rotation after shutdown.

7. LUBE OIL SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
7.1 Lube oil system shall conform to API 614 Chapter 1 and 3.
7.2 System shall include: main oil pump (shaft-driven), standby oil pump
    (motor-driven), oil reservoir (minimum 5-minute hold-up), dual oil coolers
    (100% standby), dual oil filters (changeable under pressure), oil conditioner
    unit (electrostatic precipitator type), and all instrumentation.
7.3 Lube oil specification: Turbine oil ISO VG 46. Vendor shall confirm
    compatibility with all bearings and seals in the package.
7.4 Reservoir shall be internally coated with two-component epoxy coating,
    minimum 200 microns DFT.
7.5 Oil coolers shall be of the shell-and-tube type, ASME U-stamp, with
    cooling water on the tube side to facilitate cleaning.
7.6 Oil supply temperature to bearings: 45°C ±3°C. Vendor shall size
    coolers and confirm temperature control valve Cv.
7.7 Oil supply pressure at compressor bearings: minimum 1.0 barg.
7.8 Low-low oil pressure trip setpoint shall be set at 0.5 barg.
7.9 High oil temperature alarm: 60°C. High-high trip: 65°C.

8. DRY GAS SEAL SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
8.1 Dry gas seals shall be tandem-type (primary + secondary) per API 692.
8.2 Seal gas supply: clean, dry process gas or N2 (instrument grade 99.998%)
    as primary seal gas at a pressure of at least 0.5 bar above the process
    gas pressure at the seal.
8.3 Primary seal gas flow: maximum 50 Nm³/h per seal at rated conditions.
    Vendor to confirm by calculation and supply control/monitoring system.
8.4 Seal gas panel shall include: dual filters (automatic switchover), flow
    indicators, pressure indicators, and differential pressure switches.
8.5 The secondary containment gas shall be N2 at 0.5 barg from Purchaser's
    utility header.
8.6 Gas seal vent shall be routed to the flare header (by Purchaser); Vendor to
    confirm required vent pipe size based on maximum seal leak rate.
8.7 Seal supplier shall be Vendor's standard proven seal supplier with at least
    5 years' service history in ethylene plant service. Purchaser reserves the
    right to approve seal supplier selection.

9. PROCESS GAS COOLERS
9.1 Coolers shall be shell-and-tube heat exchangers, TEMA type BEU or AEU,
    ASME Section VIII Div.1 U-stamp.
9.2 Process gas shall be on the shell side. Cooling water on tube side.
9.3 Cooling water inlet: 32°C / outlet: maximum 45°C.
9.4 Minimum approach temperature on process gas side: 5°C above cooling
    water inlet temperature.
9.5 Tube material: ASTM A249 TP316L for all coolers.
9.6 Cooler design shall include provisions for CIP (Clean-In-Place) with
    N2 purge inlet and drain nozzles.
9.7 Fouling factors per TEMA, Tables 5.1 and 5.2, plus an additional 25%
    margin on the calculated dirty U-value.

10. INSTRUMENTATION AND CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
10.1 Machine monitoring system (MMS) shall be a dedicated, stand-alone system
     per API 670. The MMS shall not be integrated with the DCS.
10.2 All transmitters shall be HART-compatible. 4-20 mA output to DCS.
10.3 Speed transmitters: three (3) per shaft, voting 2-out-of-3 logic for trip.
10.4 Vibration probes: eddy-current type, two (2) radial + one (1) axial per
     bearing housing.
10.5 Process connection instrument flanges shall be ASME B16.5 Class 300 RF.
     Thread connections are not acceptable for process measurements.
10.6 The local control panel (LCP) shall be stainless steel (316SS), IP65
     minimum, and shall contain the PLC, HMI touchscreen (minimum 15"),
     MMS, and all local controls.
10.7 Anti-surge control shall use a dedicated anti-surge controller (ASC),
     not implemented in the DCS. Vendor to supply ASC as part of LCP.
10.8 Vendor shall provide full cause-and-effect matrix and interlock logic
     diagram for Purchaser's review and approval before software coding.
10.9 All signal cables within the package shall be in metallic conduit.
     Flexible conduit shall be limited to final 500 mm connections only.
10.10 Vendor shall provide a DCS interface description document listing all
      hardwired trip signals, 4-20 mA signals, and optional Modbus/Profibus
      communication points.

11. INSPECTION AND TESTING REQUIREMENTS
11.1 Vendor shall submit ITP within 21 days of order. ITP requires written
     approval before any manufacturing begins.
11.2 Compressor mechanical test requirements (Hold points = H, Witness = W):
     a) Casing hydrostatic test at 1.5× MAWP: H
     b) Rotor balance check after final assembly: H
     c) Rotor overspeed test (115% MCS): H
     d) Mechanical run test (API 617 Clause 8.3.3, minimum 4 hours): H
     e) Performance test (API 617 Clause 8.3.4): H
     f) Vibration and bearing temperature confirmation during full-load test: H
     g) Seal gas consumption measurement during performance test: W
     h) Strip-down inspection after mechanical run test: H
     i) String test (compressor + gearbox + driver assembled): H
11.3 Gearbox: Full-load Factory Acceptance Test (FAT), all hold points per
     API 613 test requirements.
11.4 Steam turbine: Full-load test at Vendor's test facility or sub-vendor
     facility with 48-hour advance notice to Purchaser.
11.5 Lube oil system: Cleanliness flushing to ISO 4406 cleanliness level 16/14/11
     minimum; to be demonstrated by particle count analysis submitted to Purchaser.
11.6 Gas coolers: Hydrostatic test on both shell and tube side, witnessed.
11.7 Control panel (LCP): Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) including full
     simulation of all interlock and ESD functions. Duration: minimum 2 days.
     Purchaser to participate. Vendor to provide FAT procedure 2 weeks in advance.
11.8 Purchaser reserves the right to conduct supplementary third-party inspection
     at any time at any fabrication facility (including sub-vendors) with minimum
     24 hours advance notice.

13. PAINTING AND SURFACE TREATMENT
13.1 Surface preparation: Sa 2.5 per ISO 8501-1 (near-white blast cleaning).
     Sa 3 is required for all lube oil reservoir internal surfaces.
13.2 Paint system for external carbon steel surfaces:
     Primer     : Epoxy zinc-rich, DFT ≥ 75 µm
     Intermediate: Epoxy MIO (micaceous iron oxide), DFT ≥ 100 µm
     Topcoat    : Aliphatic polyurethane, DFT ≥ 75 µm
     Total DFT  : Minimum 250 µm
     Final color: RAL 9002 (grey white) for package; RAL 3000 (flame red)
                  for fire-fighting connections only.
13.3 Stainless steel and non-ferrous surfaces shall not be painted.
13.4 All machined mating surfaces and flange faces shall be protected with
     Tectyl 511M or approved equivalent. Plastic flange protectors are
     not acceptable; steel blank flanges required.
13.5 Internal carbon steel surfaces of lube oil system: Two-coat epoxy,
     minimum 200 µm DFT, non-toxic and compatible with ISO VG 46 turbine oil.

14. PRESERVATION AND PACKING
14.1 Prior to shipment, all gas-path internals shall be purged with dry N2
     and sealed with nitrogen blanket (positive N2 pressure 0.2 barg) for
     ocean freight.
14.2 Lube oil system shall be flushed, drained, and internally preserved
     with oil mist or VCI (Vapor Corrosion Inhibitor) capsules.
14.3 All external shafts, machined surfaces, and threaded connections shall
     be coated with long-term rust inhibitor and covered.
14.4 All instruments and electrical equipment shall be individually wrapped
     in moisture-absorbing packaging and packed in foam-padded cases.
14.5 The complete package shall be mounted on a heavy-duty steel skid and
     crated with fully enclosed weather-proof wooden crating for ocean freight
     (ISPM 15 heat-treated timber required).
14.6 Maximum individual lift weight: 30 metric tons. If the package exceeds
     this limit, Vendor shall propose lifting arrangement and confirm with
     Purchaser's site rigging contractor before shipment.
14.7 Packing list shall be supplied in triplicate showing: item description,
     quantity, weight (net/gross), dimensions, and equipment tag number.

15. SPARE PARTS
15.1 Commissioning spares (24-month operation, included in base price):
     a) Complete set of shaft end seals
     b) All instrument tubing fittings and O-rings × 2 sets
     c) Lube oil filter elements × 12
     d) Seal gas filter elements × 6
     e) Complete coupling spacer assembly × 1
     f) All gaskets for each flanged connection × 2 sets
15.2 Installed spare rotor (as listed in 1.1(n)):
     Spare rotor shall be fully assembled with impellers, sleeves, and seals,
     ready to install. It shall be preserved and stored in dedicated transport
     frame with N2 blanket.
15.3 Vendor shall submit a recommended 2-year and 5-year spare parts list
     with unit prices at time of bid. This list shall not be binding but serves
     as a reference for Purchaser's long-term spares procurement planning.

17. HEALTH, SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENT (HSE) REQUIREMENTS
17.1 Noise level shall not exceed 85 dB(A) at 1 m from the package
     boundary under rated operating conditions.
     If this limit cannot be met, Vendor shall propose an acoustic enclosure.
17.2 All lifting attachments (lugs, trunnions) shall be proof-tested to 150%
     of maximum lift weight and certified by an independent inspection body.
17.3 Vendor's manufacturing facility shall hold ISO 45001 (or OHSAS 18001)
     and ISO 14001 certifications. Certificates to be submitted at bid.
17.4 No asbestos-containing materials shall be used in any part of the package.
     Vendor to provide declaration of compliance.
17.5 All grease fittings shall be of type compatible with bio-degradable grease
     per ISO 15380 Type L-EAL.
