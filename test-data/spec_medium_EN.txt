TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION
CENTRIFUGAL PUMP PACKAGE – CRUDE OIL FEED SERVICE
================================================================================

Project       : Nippon Refinery Expansion Project Phase 2
Purchaser     : Nippon Petroleum Refining Co., Ltd.
Equipment Tag : 12-P-101 A/B
Quantity      : 2 sets (1 operating + 1 standby)
Date          : April 2026

1. SCOPE OF SUPPLY
1.1 The Vendor shall supply a complete centrifugal pump package in accordance with
    this specification, including pump, base plate, coupling, coupling guard, and
    all appurtenances necessary for a complete and operable unit.
1.2 The supply shall include all instruments mounted on the package as listed in
    the Instrument Index (Attachment A).
1.3 Commissioning spare parts for 2 years of continuous operation shall be included
    in the scope.
1.4 All special tools required for maintenance shall be included.

2. APPLICABLE CODES AND STANDARDS
2.1 The pump shall be designed and manufactured in full conformance with
    API 610, 12th Edition (latest edition at time of order placement).
2.2 Pressure-containing parts shall conform to ASME Section VIII Division 1.
2.3 All flanges shall be ASME B16.5 Class 300 rating minimum.
2.4 Electrical equipment in the vicinity of the pump shall comply with IEC 60079
    (Zone 1 classification).
2.5 All materials shall be fully traceable and certified to EN 10204 Type 3.1
    material test reports.
2.6 Where conflicts exist between codes, the more stringent requirement shall apply.
    Any conflicts shall be reported to Purchaser in writing within 14 days of
    order placement.

3. DESIGN CONDITIONS
3.1 Fluid                    : Crude oil (API gravity 28–34)
3.2 Rated flow               : 450 m³/h
3.3 Differential head        : 180 m
3.4 Suction pressure         : 0.5 barg (min) / 2.0 barg (max)
3.5 Discharge pressure       : 19.5 barg
3.6 Operating temperature    : 60°C normal / 80°C maximum
3.7 Minimum operating temperature : -10°C (winter startup condition)
3.8 Specific gravity         : 0.87 at operating temperature
3.9 Viscosity                : 25 cSt at 60°C
3.10 Vapor pressure          : 0.08 barg at 60°C
3.11 NPSH available          : 4.5 m (minimum, at rated flow)

4. MECHANICAL DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Pump type shall be API 610 type OH2 (overhung, between bearing).
4.2 The pump shall be capable of operating continuously at any flow between
    70% and 110% of best efficiency point (BEP) without mechanical seal failure
    or excessive vibration.
4.3 The pump shall be designed for a minimum service life of 25 years.
4.4 Impeller shall be of the closed type with wear rings on both impeller and casing.
4.5 The minimum radial clearance between impeller and casing shall be 0.3 mm
    per side.
4.6 Shaft deflection at the seal faces shall not exceed 0.05 mm under any
    operating condition.
4.7 The baseplate shall be of the sole-plate type, fabricated from structural steel,
    stress-relieved, and hot-dip galvanized after fabrication.
4.8 All external bolting shall be 316 stainless steel, minimum Grade B8.
4.9 The pump unit weight shall not exceed 2,500 kg including base plate.
    If this limit cannot be met, Vendor shall notify Purchaser within 21 days
    of order.

5. MATERIAL REQUIREMENTS
5.1 Casing and cover         : ASTM A351 CF8M (316SS equivalent) minimum
5.2 Impeller                 : ASTM A351 CF8M
5.3 Shaft                    : ASTM A564 Type 630 (17-4PH, H1150 condition)
5.4 Wear rings               : ASTM A276 Type 316L with stellite overlay
5.5 Casing bolting           : ASTM A193 B8M / A194 8M
5.6 No cast iron shall be used for any pressure-containing or rotating parts.
5.7 All materials in contact with process fluid shall be suitable for H2S service
    per NACE MR0175/ISO 15156.

6. MECHANICAL SEAL REQUIREMENTS
6.1 Mechanical seal shall be API 682 Plan 32 (flush from external clean source)
    with Plan 52 (unpressurized buffer fluid) as secondary containment.
6.2 Seal flush fluid shall be provided by Purchaser at 4 barg, 40°C max.
6.3 Seal face materials: primary—silicon carbide/silicon carbide;
    secondary—carbon/silicon carbide.
6.4 Mean time between planned maintenance (MTBPM) for seal shall be minimum
    25,000 hours under rated operating conditions.
6.5 Dual mechanical seal arrangement (Type C per API 682) is mandatory.
    Single seals will not be accepted.

7. DRIVER REQUIREMENTS
7.1 Drive motor shall be supplied by Purchaser. Motor details are given in
    Attachment B (Motor Data Sheet).
7.2 Vendor shall supply coupling, coupling guard, and shaft alignment report.
7.3 Vendor shall confirm motor shaft dimensions within 14 days of order to
    allow motor procurement in parallel.
7.4 The coupling shall be of the disc-type, non-lubricated, with a service factor
    of minimum 1.5 on rated torque.

8. INSPECTION AND TESTING
8.1 Vendor shall prepare and submit an Inspection and Test Plan (ITP) within
    28 days of order placement for Purchaser's written approval.
8.2 The following tests are MANDATORY with Purchaser witness (H – Hold point):
    a) Hydrostatic test of casing at 1.5 × design pressure: H
    b) NPSH performance test per API 610 Annex F: H
    c) Complete performance test (H-Q curve, efficiency, power): H
    d) Mechanical run test at rated speed, minimum 4 hours: H
    e) Vibration measurement per API 670 during mechanical run: H
    f) API 610 Annex E strip-down inspection after mechanical run: H
8.3 All test results shall be within the tolerances of API 610 Table 2.
    Any deviation shall require re-test after Purchaser approval.
8.4 Third-party inspection by Purchaser's nominated TPI agency shall be
    accommodated at all stages of manufacture at no additional cost.
8.5 Purchaser's inspector shall have unrestricted access to the manufacturing
    facility at all times during the contract period.

9. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
9.1 All documentation shall be submitted in English language only.
9.2 Drawing approval cycle: Vendor shall resubmit within 10 working days
    of receiving Purchaser's comments.
9.3 The following documents are required for approval before manufacture:
    a) General Arrangement drawing
    b) Cross-section drawing
    c) Bill of Materials
    d) ITP
    e) Mechanical Seal data sheet
9.4 Final documentation shall be delivered as 3 hard copies + 1 electronic copy
    (PDF + native CAD format) in a Master Document Register (MDR).
9.5 All drawings shall use the Purchaser's title block format (template in
    Attachment C).
9.6 Operating and Maintenance Manual shall be submitted 4 weeks before
    shipment.

10. PAINTING, PRESERVATION AND PACKING
10.1 External surfaces shall be painted per Purchaser's paint specification
     NPR-PAINT-001 (Attachment D): DFT minimum 250 microns total,
     final color RAL 5015 (sky blue).
10.2 All machined surfaces shall be protected with temporary rust-preventive
     compound, removable by solvent cleaning.
10.3 Pump casing internals shall be flushed with clean rust inhibitor oil and
     all openings blanked with steel plates, not plastic caps.
10.4 The unit shall be crated in wooden export packing suitable for ocean
     freight. Packing shall withstand 3 months outdoor storage.
10.5 Packing marking shall include: Project name, PO number, equipment tag,
     net weight, gross weight, and center-of-gravity marking.

11. DELIVERY AND SCHEDULE
11.1 Required delivery date: within 28 weeks from the date of Purchase Order.
11.2 Vendor shall submit a detailed manufacturing schedule within 14 days of
     order, showing all critical milestones.
11.3 Liquidated damages for late delivery: 0.5% of contract value per week,
     maximum 10% total.
11.4 Vendor shall notify Purchaser in writing of any anticipated delay immediately
     upon becoming aware of it.
